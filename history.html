🔹 Origins and Early Use

Marijuana, also known as cannabis or pot, has a long history of human use... This ancient plant was used in Asia for both ritual and healing purposes, setting the foundation for centuries of cultural integration.

🔹 Hemp in Colonial America

The cannabis or hemp plant originally evolved in Central Asia... In America, it became a major agricultural product, essential for producing rope, sails, and early textiles.

🔹 Spiritual and Medicinal Practices

There’s some evidence that ancient cultures knew about the psychoactive properties... Many early societies used cannabis in religious ceremonies to enhance spiritual experiences and communion with the divine.

🔹 Rise of Medical Marijuana

In the 1830s, Sir William Brooke O’Shaughnessy, an Irish doctor studying in India... Cannabis extracts were commonly prescribed to treat pain, nausea, and neurological disorders in the 19th century.

🔹 Ancient Recreational Use

Herodotus described the Scythians—a large group of Iranian nomads... They used cannabis to relax, socialize, and experience altered states of consciousness.

🔹 Cannabis in the U.S. (1900s)

In the United States, marijuana wasn’t widely used for recreational purposes until the early 1900s... It became especially popular among jazz musicians and counterculture movements.

🔹 The Marijuana Tax Act of 1937

The Marijuana Tax Act of 1937 was the first federal U.S. law to criminalize marijuana nationwide... It marked the beginning of nationwide cannabis prohibition.

🔹 World War II and Industrial Hemp

Industrial hemp continued to be grown in the United States throughout World War II... Farmers were encouraged to cultivate hemp for military use under the “Hemp for Victory” campaign.

🔹 Controlled Substances Act & War on Drugs

As part of the “War on Drugs,” the Controlled Substances Act of 1970... Cannabis was listed as a Schedule I drug, despite scientific and medical objections.

🔹 The Shafer Report & Early Advocacy

In 1972, a report from the National Commission on Marijuana and Drug Abuse... The report actually recommended decriminalization, but the advice was ignored by the Nixon administration.

🔹 Legalization Movement

California, in the Compassionate Use Act of 1996, became the first state to legalize marijuana for medicinal use... This set a precedent for a nationwide shift in cannabis policy.

🔹 Modern Use and Effects

Adults also can light up without a doctor’s prescription in many states... Cannabis is now widely recognized for both its recreational and therapeutic benefits.

🔹 Potency Trends Over Time

The amount of THC in marijuana—the chemical responsible for the drug’s potency—has increased dramatically... Modern strains can contain up to 30% THC, a stark contrast to older generations.
