Cannabis has a rich history dating back thousands of years. First cultivated in Central Asia, it was used for fiber, food, and medicine. Ancient Chinese texts mention cannabis as early as 2700 BCE, where it was valued for its healing properties.

In ancient India, cannabis was revered as one of the five sacred plants. It appeared in religious rituals and traditional Ayurvedic medicine. Meanwhile, the Scythians, an ancient Eurasian nomadic tribe, burned cannabis seeds in ceremonial tents, spreading its use across Europe.

By the 19th century, Western medicine began using cannabis extracts to treat conditions like pain, insomnia, and nausea. However, due to political pressure and racialized propaganda, cannabis was criminalized across much of the world by the mid-20th century.
